For Residents

The purpose of the Questionnaire is to learn more about the history of Old Smokey, the former incinerator, which operated from 1925 to 1970, and its environmental health impact on the West Grove community.

FAQs

Q1: How can you get involved with the Old Smokey Steering Committee?

A: If you are interested in joining or learning more about the OSSC, please contact oldsmokeysteeringcommittee@gmail.com.

Q2: What contaminants have been found?

A: Contaminants include arsenic, antimony, barium, lead, PAH’s, and PCB’s.

Q3: Where has contamination been found?

A: Contamination has been found at the former site of Old Smokey (currently the Fire Training College), offsite locations adjacent to the former incinerator, and an additional 8 City of Miami parks:

Q4: What has the local government been doing to clean up the contamination?

A: The local government is in the process of assessing the level of contamination and developing remediation plans for each of the parks and the area surrounding the Fire Training College. As of March 2015, only Blanche Park and Merrie Christmas Park’s remediation plans have been completed. For more information on the status of the remaining contaminated sites, visit derm.miamidade.gov and contact your local representatives.
